
# Reference:

Bonandrini, R., Baayen, R.H., & Marelli, M. (under review). Between semasiology and onomasiology: leveraging meaning-form mapping to create novel words with a predefined meaning. https://osf.io/hqut8/ 

# Data source:

https://osf.io/hqut8/

# Description: 

We explored whether regularities between form and meaning estimated through Linear Discriminative Learning can be used to create novel words that convey a desired meaning. 

## Experiment 1:

- participants had to identify a generated pseudoword resulting from one single word prompt (i.e., a “pseudo-synonym”). The study was conducted in Italian on Italian speakers.
- 2695 datapoints from 34 participants

## Experiment 2:

- participants had to identify a pair of word prompts whose combined meaning produced a certain pseudoword. The study was conducted in Italian on Italian speakers.
- 2721 datapoints from 34 participants

## Experiment 3:

- participants had to identify a generated pseudoword resulting from the combined meaning of a pair of word prompts. The study was conducted in Italian on Italian speakers.
- 2733 datapoints from 34 participants
