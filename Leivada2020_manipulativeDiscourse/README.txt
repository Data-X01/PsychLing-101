General information

The study Bilinguals Are Better Than Monolinguals in Detecting Manipulative Discourse compares the performance of monolingual and bilingual participants on an identical evaluation task. The dataset is divided into two files, “monolinguals” and “bilinguals,” corresponding to the two participant groups.

All participants completed the same task, which consisted of 30 items. For each item, participants were required to assess a statement and classify it as Correct, Wrong, or Neither correct nor wrong.

Additional details regarding the dataset and methodology are available in the repository maintained by the Universitat Rovira i Virgili (URV).
Link can be found here: https://repositori.urv.cat/estatic/PC0041/ca_PC3577.html

Also, the full article can be found here: https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0256173
